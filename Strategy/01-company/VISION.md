# Company Vision

## 1. Purpose
Paints a vivid picture of the long-term future the company aims to achieve. It serves as the "North Star" for all long-term architectural scaling and business planning.

## 2. Why It Matters
While the Mission dictates *what* we do today, the Vision dictates *where* we must be able to scale tomorrow. If the vision includes "Global academic esports tournaments," the architecture must be designed today to handle cross-region matchmaking and massive spectator concurrency.

## 3. Example Structure
- **1-Year Vision**: Immediate market capture.
- **3-Year Vision**: Platform expansion and ecosystem growth.
- **10-Year Vision**: The ultimate industry-altering goal.

## 4. Example Content
**1-Year Vision**: Become the standard competitive trivia platform for top engineering colleges in the region.
**3-Year Vision**: Expand to all academic disciplines, integrate directly with institutional grading systems, and host inter-university national tournaments.
**10-Year Vision**: Replace standardized testing with dynamic, verifiable ELO rankings that employers use to recruit top talent globally.

## 5. AI Usage Instructions
> [!TIP]  
> When tasked with designing database schemas or microservices, the AI must ensure the design does not create a technical dead-end that blocks the 3-Year Vision (e.g., hardcoding single-university constraints).

## 6. Developer Usage Instructions
- Let the vision inspire your architectural headroom. Build for the current sprint, but leave interfaces open for future global scale.

## 7. Best Practices
- **Do**: Be ambitious. The vision should sound slightly impossible.
- **Don't**: Confuse Vision with Product Roadmap. The vision is the destination; the roadmap is the path.

## 8. Maintenance Strategy
- **Owner**: CEO / Founders.
- **Update Frequency**: Annually.
- **Trigger**: Annual strategic planning retreats.
