# Long-Term Roadmap

## 1. Purpose
Maps the multi-year trajectory of the company. Unlike the `PRODUCT-ROADMAP.md` (which covers the next 3-12 months of tangible software), this covers Years 2 through 5.

## 2. Why It Matters
Investors, enterprise clients, and senior engineers need to know the company isn't just a single-feature app. A long-term roadmap proves the platform is evolving toward a massive, defensible ecosystem.

## 3. Example Structure
- **Year 1**: The current foundation.
- **Year 2-3**: Platform expansion.
- **Year 4-5**: Ecosystem dominance.

## 4. Example Content
**Year 1: The Core Arena (Current)**
- Perfect the 1v1 PvP Trivia Engine.
- Capture the initial university market.
- Establish baseline ELO ranking algorithms.

**Years 2-3: The Institutional Hub**
- Deep LMS Integration (Canvas, Blackboard via LTI).
- Move beyond Trivia into long-form assessment (Coding, Essays).
- Launch the "EduRank Recruiter Portal" allowing top tech companies to hire Transistor-tier students directly based on verifiable data.

**Years 4-5: The Global Standard**
- Replace legacy standardized testing (GRE, SAT) with dynamic, continuous ELO assessment.
- Host global academic esports tournaments with physical arenas and sponsorships.

## 5. AI Usage Instructions
> [!IMPORTANT]  
> AI agents should use this document to understand the ultimate, massive scale of the product. It contextualizes why the system must be built with enterprise-grade modularity from Day 1.

## 6. Developer Usage Instructions
- Do not let the 5-Year plan distract you from the current sprint.
- Use this document to motivate yourself and understand the impact of the foundation you are currently laying.

## 7. Best Practices
- **Do**: Tie the milestones directly to the `VISION.md`.
- **Don't**: Put specific dates or technological commitments here.

## 8. Maintenance Strategy
- **Owner**: CEO / Board of Directors.
- **Update Frequency**: Annually.
- **Trigger**: Reaching major company milestones or finalizing new rounds of venture funding.
